A browser-based application that stores contact information locally. Data persists even after closing the browser.

How This Works

This application uses the browser's localStorage API to store contact data. Here's what happens:

  • When you add a contact, it's saved to localStorage in your browser.
  • The data persists even after closing the browser or restarting your computer.
  • When the page loads, it automatically retrieves and displays all stored contacts.
  • Each contact contains: ID, Name, Telephone Number, and Address.
  • The sample data button pre-loads 5 example contacts, including Layla's contact.

How to Test Before Sending to Your Professor

  • Test adding contacts: Fill out the form and click "Save Contact"
  • Test persistence: Close the browser tab, then reopen it to see if contacts are still there
  • Try the sample data: Click "Load Sample Data" to see 5 pre-filled contacts including Layla's
  • Check browser developer tools: Press F12, go to the "Application" tab, and look under "Local Storage" to see the raw stored data
  • Test on different devices: Open the link on your phone or another computer to confirm data is stored locally on each device
